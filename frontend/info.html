Menstrual Health Information

Want to know more about menstrual cycle phases or PCOS/PCOD? Read on!


The menstrual cycle is a complex and natural process that prepares a woman's body for pregnancy.
It is typically divided into four main phases:

  • the menstrual phase
  • the follicular phase
  • ovulation
  • the luteal phase

Each phase is characterized by distinct hormonal changes and physiological responses.
The menstrual phase marks the beginning of the cycle, where the uterine lining sheds if pregnancy has not occurred. This period is frequently called the "monthly periods". It typically lasts around one week, but varies among individuals. This is followed by the follicular phase, where the body prepares an egg for release. Ovulation occurs mid-cycle, releasing the mature egg, which is then followed by the luteal phase, where the body readies itself for potential pregnancy. The entire cycle usually spans the period of approximately one month.
Understanding these phases is crucial for managing reproductive health, as well as creating diet and exercise routines to align with the body's natural rhythms.
